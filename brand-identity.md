# Peanut Gallery - Brand Identity

> _"Where honest feedback meets creative courage"_

---

## Brand Personality

**1. Authentic**  
We don't sugarcoat. We don't perform. We speak truth with warmth, not cruelty. Real conversations, real people, real impact.

**2. Playful**  
We take our work seriously, not ourselves. There's room for humor, experimentation, and the occasional dad joke. Serious doesn't mean stiff.

**3. Insightful**  
We cut through noise to find signal. Every observation has depth. Every opinion is grounded in understanding, not hot takes.

**4. Inclusive**  
Everyone has a seat at the gallery. We amplify diverse voices, welcome fresh perspectives, and believe the best ideas come from the edges, not the center.

**5. Bold**  
We'd rather be interesting than safe. We challenge conventions, ask uncomfortable questions, and champion ideas that matter over ideas that polish.

## Tone of Voice Guidelines

### Speaking to Marketers

**Do:**
- Lead with outcomes and impact, not just features
- Use vivid, concrete language that paints a picture
- Acknowledge the real challenges they face (budget constraints, stakeholder alignment, proving ROI)
- Balance strategy with tactical actionable insights
- Show, don't tell — use examples, case studies, before/after scenarios

**Don't:**
- Drown them in jargon or buzzwords
- Talk down about "vanity metrics" — their KPIs matter to them
- Assume they're shallow or only care about surface-level wins
- Overpromise or hype without substance

**Example:**
> ❌ "Our platform leverages AI-driven synergies to optimize your marketing funnel."
>
> ✅ "Here's what actually moves the needle: We helped a B2B SaaS company cut their CAC by 34% in 90 days by fixing their onboarding flow. Let's talk about what's broken in yours."

---

### Speaking to Developers

**Do:**
- Respect their expertise and time
- Be precise and technical when it matters
- Acknowledge trade-offs — nothing is perfect
- Show the "why" behind recommendations, not just the "what"
- Use real code, real examples, real constraints

**Don't:**
- Oversimplify or dumb things down
- Hand-wave technical details with "magic happens here"
- Treat them as order-takers rather than problem-solvers
- Sell them buzzword architecture without substance

**Example:**
> ❌ "Just plug in our API and watch the magic happen!"
>
> ✅ "Here's the webhook payload structure. You'll need to handle retries on 5xx errors — we recommend exponential backoff with a 30s cap. Here's a sample implementation in Node."

---

### Universal Principles

- **Conversational, not casual** — We sound like humans, but we're not sloppy
- **Confident, not arrogant** — We know our stuff, but we're always learning
- **Direct, not blunt** — Honesty doesn't require harshness
- **Warm, not corporate** — We're approachable without being unprofessional

## Color Palette

### Primary Colors

| Color | Hex | Usage | Rationale |
|-------|-----|-------|-----------|
| **Gallery White** | `#F8F7F4` | Backgrounds, canvas space | Warm off-white evokes actual gallery walls — clean but not sterile, inviting not clinical |
| **Peanut Brown** | `#8B6F4E` | Primary brand color, headers | Earthy, grounded, approachable. References the "peanut" without being literal or cartoonish |
| **Deep Charcoal** | `#2D2D2D` | Body text, primary contrast | Softer than pure black, easier on the eyes, maintains sophistication |

### Accent Colors

| Color | Hex | Usage | Rationale |
|-------|-----|-------|-----------|
| **Critique Red** | `#C44536` | CTAs, alerts, important highlights | Attention-grabbing without being alarming. Says "look here" not "danger" |
| **Insight Blue** | `#4A7C9D` | Links, secondary actions, trust signals | Calm, trustworthy, professional. Balances the warmth of the browns |
| **Fresh Green** | `#6B8E5E` | Success states, positive metrics, growth | Natural, organic growth. Not neon, not corporate — muted and genuine |

### Neutral Palette

| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Warm Gray Light | `#E8E4DF` | Borders, dividers, subtle backgrounds |
| Warm Gray Mid | `#A8A29A` | Secondary text, icons, disabled states |
| Warm Gray Dark | `#5C5650` | Tertiary text, subtle emphasis |

### Accessibility Notes

- All text meets WCAG AA contrast minimums (4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text)
- Primary CTA (Critique Red on Gallery White): 7.2:1 contrast ratio
- Never use Insight Blue or Fresh Green for critical text — reserve for decorative/secondary use

## Typography Recommendations

### Primary Font (Headings & Display)

**Font:** **Space Grotesk** (Google Fonts — Free)  
**Why:** Quirky geometric sans-serif with personality. Technical enough for devs, interesting enough for marketers. The slightly unconventional letterforms signal "we think differently" without being unreadable.

**Usage:**
- H1: 48px / 600 (SemiBold)
- H2: 36px / 600 (SemiBold)
- H3: 24px / 500 (Medium)
- H4: 20px / 500 (Medium)

**Fallback:** `Space Grotesk, "Segoe UI", system-ui, sans-serif`

---

### Secondary Font (Body & UI)

**Font:** **Inter** (Google Fonts — Free)  
**Why:** The workhorse. Incredibly readable at any size, neutral enough to let content shine, optimized for screens. Pairs beautifully with Space Grotesk's personality.

**Usage:**
- Body: 16px / 400 (Regular), line-height: 1.6
- Small/Caption: 14px / 400 (Regular)
- UI Labels: 14px / 500 (Medium)
- Code Inline: 14px / 400 (Regular), use `Inter` with monospace fallback for consistency

**Fallback:** `Inter, "Segoe UI", system-ui, sans-serif`

---

### Monospace (Code & Technical)

**Font:** **JetBrains Mono** (Google Fonts — Free)  
**Why:** Designed for developers. Excellent character differentiation, ligatures for common operators, comfortable for long reading sessions.

**Usage:**
- Code blocks: 14px / 400 (Regular)
- Inline code: 13px / 400 (Regular)
- Technical specs, API docs, data

**Fallback:** `JetBrains Mono, "Fira Code", "Consolas", monospace`

---

### Typography Principles

- **Hierarchy through size and weight, not font changes** — Stick to the system
- **Generous whitespace** — Let content breathe. Gallery walls aren't cluttered.
- **Left-aligned body text** — Easier to read, more natural eye movement
- **Max line length: 75 characters** — Optimal readability
- **Avoid all-caps** — Except for very short labels (1-3 words)

## Logo Concepts

### Concept 1: **The Spotlight**

**Description:** A minimalist spotlight beam forming a subtle "P" shape. The beam illuminates a small circle (the "peanut") in an otherwise dark gallery space.

**Vibe:** Sophisticated, theatrical, focused. Says "we shine light on what matters."

**Best for:** Premium positioning, B2B enterprise focus, conference materials

**Considerations:** May feel too formal for casual contexts. Works best in monochrome or two-color applications.

---

### Concept 2: **The Conversation Bubble**

**Description:** Two overlapping speech bubbles — one larger (marketer), one smaller (dev) — forming a peanut shape in the negative space where they intersect.

**Vibe:** Collaborative, dialogue-focused, clever. Says "we facilitate conversations between worlds."

**Best for:** Community-building, educational content, social media

**Considerations:** Requires careful execution to ensure the peanut shape is visible. May not scale well to very small sizes.

---

### Concept 3: **The Gallery Frame**

**Description:** A simple square frame (like a gallery picture frame) with a stylized peanut icon centered inside. Clean, iconic, instantly recognizable.

**Vibe:** Classic, timeless, confident. Says "we curate what's worth displaying."

**Best for:** Versatile applications, favicon/app icon, merchandise

**Considerations:** Risk of feeling generic. Needs strong color or texture treatment to stand out.

---

### Concept 4: **The Megaphone Peanut**

**Description:** A peanut character (abstract, not cartoonish) holding or integrated with a megaphone. The peanut _is_ the voice.

**Vibe:** Bold, playful, activist-adjacent. Says "we amplify what deserves to be heard."

**Best for:** Thought leadership, contrarian positioning, younger demographics

**Considerations:** Walks the line between playful and gimmicky. Requires skilled illustration to avoid feeling juvenile.

---

### Concept 5: **The Wordmark**

**Description:** "Peanut Gallery" set in custom lettering — Space Grotesk modified with subtle peanut-shell textures or gallery-column influences integrated into the letterforms.

**Vibe:** Modern, design-forward, confident. Says "we are the brand — no symbol needed."

**Best for:** Digital-first applications, headlines, contexts where the name is already known

**Considerations:** Less recognizable at small sizes. Requires strong brand recall to work standalone.

---

### Logo Usage Guidelines

- **Minimum size:** 24px height for full logo, 16px for icon-only
- **Clear space:** Maintain 1x logo height of clear space on all sides
- **Never stretch or distort** — Use locked aspect ratio
- **Color variations:** Full color (primary), single color (Peanut Brown or Deep Charcoal), reversed (white on dark)
- **Don'ts:** No drop shadows, no gradients, no outlines, no placing on busy backgrounds without a solid backdrop

## Final Notes

This brand identity is designed to be:

- **Flexible** — Works across digital, print, events, and merchandise
- **Memorable** — Distinctive without being gimmicky
- **Scalable** — Grows with the brand from startup to enterprise
